01 — why bother

The problem was never that you're lazy.

It's that the tools were built for someone else. A normal timer is a number shrinking in the corner, and you don't feel a number. A normal to-do app wants you to build a whole system first, so building the system quietly becomes the thing you avoid. And the streak counter? The day you slip, it drops you to zero and you never open it again. None of that is a focus problem. It's a design problem, and design problems can be fixed.

02 — what it does

Five things, done properly.

No swiss-army app. These are the five things people with ADHD keep asking for, so that's all FocusKit is. Each one earns its place.

A companion that sits with you

Working next to another person makes the impossible suddenly doable. Body doubling is the closest thing to a cheat code the ADHD brain has. FocusKit's companion stays with you for the whole session, so the work never feels like grinding alone. No call to join. No stranger on a webcam. Just the quiet sense that someone's there.

Catch the thought in one tap

The idea shows up, you tap once, it's saved. No folders, no tags, no project to maintain. If capture takes longer than two seconds, the thought's already gone.

Progress that won't punish you

It counts the days you showed up. That's the whole metric. Miss a day and nothing resets, nothing guilts you. Streaks that punish you are the reason you quit the last app.

Four lengths, no setup

5, 15, 25, or 45 minutes. Pick one, start. A soft sound ends it, never an alarm-clock jolt.

Built to stay quiet

High-contrast and reduce-motion are on day one. Calm colors. No badges, no popup begging for a rating.

Yours, and only yours

Everything lives on your phone. Nothing leaves it. The next card is the part I care about most.

03 — the part I won't compromise

It stays on your phone. All of it.

Local-first, and I mean it literally. No account to make, no password, nothing syncing to a server you'd have to trust me with. There's no ad SDK buried in it and nothing logging what you do. The whole thing is source-available, so you don't have to believe a word of this. Go read every line.
