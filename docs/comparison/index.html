ADHD focus apps compared: what each one actually does

No affiliate links, no sponsored placements. Just a direct comparison of the apps the ADHD community uses most, written from reading the same community threads that shaped this category.

By NyxJune 20269 min read

The ADHD focus app space is full of products that either copy the standard productivity playbook (timer + task list + streaks) or take the ADHD branding seriously. The gap between those two is where most people drop out. This comparison is about that gap.

The apps covered here are the ones that come up repeatedly in the threads: Focusmate, Forest, Tiimo, and a newer entrant, FocusKit. I've tried to be fair about what each one does well — this isn't a hit piece.

The short version

If you need real human body doubling via video: Focusmate is the best answer, by a wide margin. If you want gamification that actually helps you start tasks: Forest is good at what it does. If you need a visual daily planner for your whole day: Tiimo is the most ADHD-specific of the complex-planner category. If you want body doubling without the video call, a visual time-blindness timer, and nothing else: FocusKit is the minimalist alternative.

Focusmate

Focusmate
Web / iOS / Android · Founded 2017 · Subscription
$6.99 / month

Video-based virtual co-working. You book a session, get matched with a stranger, turn on your camera, both say what you're working on, work silently, and check in at the end. It's the original body doubling app, and it still works better than almost anything else for raw accountability.

Works well
  • Real accountability with a real human
  • Massive community — sessions available 24/7
  • The check-in structure provides closure
  • Proven: thousands of testimonials from ADHD adults
Friction points
  • Must schedule ahead — no "I need it now" option
  • Camera required (not always sensory-safe)
  • 25/50 min fixed sessions — no custom length
  • Social overhead can itself be a barrier
need real human presence structured session format can plan ahead

The thing Focusmate gets right is that body doubling with a real person is categorically different from an app simulating presence. If the video call format works for you and you're not sensory-averse to cameras, it's genuinely the most effective option. The cost is the overhead: you have to schedule, show up at the right time, and be camera-ready. For anyone who struggles with transitions and scheduling, that's a meaningful barrier.

Forest

Forest
iOS / Android · Gamified focus timer
Free / $1.99 unlock

Plant a virtual tree, and it grows while you focus. Leave the app and the tree dies. Earn coins to plant real trees. One of the most downloaded focus apps — and not specifically designed for ADHD.

Works well
  • Gentle loss-aversion mechanic actually gets people to stay
  • Clean, calm UI — not overwhelming
  • Real trees planted (surprisingly motivating)
  • Cheap and available on Android
Friction points
  • Not designed with ADHD in mind at all
  • No body doubling
  • No time blindness support — standard countdown
  • Dead tree = shame mechanics
phone-lock discipline light gamification not specifically ADHD

Forest is a good tool for one specific problem: stopping yourself from picking up your phone during a session. The loss-aversion mechanic (don't kill your tree) is psychologically sound. But it doesn't address time blindness, doesn't provide any body doubling, and the dead-tree shame cycle is exactly the pattern that makes ADHD apps unsustainable long-term. It's the most popular app in this category because it's cheap and visually satisfying — not because it's built for ADHD.

Tiimo

Tiimo
iOS / Android · ADHD / autism daily planner
$4.99 / month

A visual daily planner designed specifically for ADHD and autism. You structure your whole day in visual blocks, with timer countdowns and gentle reminders. It's the most genuinely ADHD-designed of the complex-planner category.

Works well
  • Genuinely designed with ADHD in mind
  • Visual daily overview helps with time blindness
  • Customizable activity timers and reminders
  • Praised by occupational therapists
Friction points
  • Requires building and maintaining a full schedule
  • High setup cost — counterintuitive for ADHD brains
  • Overkill if you only need a focus timer
  • Monthly subscription for a planner
daily structure and planning ADHD + autism overlap full-day visual scheduling

Tiimo is the right answer if your problem is daily structure — getting your whole day planned in a way your brain can process visually. It's more of a planner than a focus tool, which is both its strength and its limitation. If you need to manage a full schedule with reminders and visual time blocks, it's genuinely excellent. If you just need to focus on one thing for 25 minutes right now, it's more tool than you need.

"Tiimo was too much to set up and I stopped using it after a week. I needed something I could open when I was already in distress, not something I had to maintain."

FocusKit

FocusKit
Android · Local-first · Launching June 24, 2026
Free to start

A deliberately minimal ADHD focus app for Android. Five features — body-doubling companion, visual time-blindness timer, one-tap task capture, no-shame progress, sensory-calm design — and nothing extra. Local-first, no account, no ads.

Works well
  • No scheduling — body doubling on demand, any time
  • Timer is a visual ring that depletes (not digits)
  • Zero setup, zero account — open and use it
  • No streak mechanics, no shame design
Limitations
  • No real human body doubling (companion is simulated)
  • Android only (no iOS yet)
  • Very new — v1 is deliberately bare-bones
  • No cloud sync or backup yet
body doubling without video calls time blindness support privacy-first / no account minimal, low-friction

Which one is right for you

Use Focusmate if:

You do your best work alongside a real person, you're comfortable on camera, and you can plan your body doubling sessions in advance. The accountability is real. Nothing simulates it better. Worth every penny of the subscription if it works for you.

Use Forest if:

Your main problem is picking up your phone during a session. The loss-aversion mechanic is genuinely effective for phone distraction specifically. It's not a body doubling app and it's not designed for ADHD, but it's simple and it works for what it does.

Use Tiimo if:

You need to structure your whole day, not just a focus session. If your ADHD manifests as "I don't know what to do with my time" rather than "I know what to do but can't start," Tiimo is built for you.

Use FocusKit if:

You want body doubling on demand without a video call or scheduled session, you lose track of time (not just digits, but the actual feeling of it passing), and you don't want to create an account or hand your data to anyone. Especially relevant if you've deleted other apps after breaking a streak — FocusKit doesn't have streaks at all.

The honest answer is that most people with ADHD try several of these and use different ones at different times. They're not mutually exclusive. But if you're looking for one to start with and your core problem is "I can't start tasks alone," go with Focusmate or FocusKit depending on whether you want real human presence or zero-friction on-demand.
